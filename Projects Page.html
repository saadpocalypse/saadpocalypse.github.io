RŌZ

RŌZ was part of the joint effort by my team and I in Microsofot's Imagine Cup 2021, where it got us a spot in the world finals and a victory in the inaugural Epic Challenge. Currently in its clinical trials phase, RŌZ will soon be available in stores near you, taking its part in reducing deaths from breast cancer all over the world. Sudoku and Word Search

Sudoku and Word Search were part of a bonus assignment in my Theory of Automata and Design and Analysis of Algorithms course. Sudoku gives the user the option to choose between 5 board variations. The user chooses the cell to input a number in by giving the row and column number and can check what rows, columns or grids the errors are present in. In Word Search, a 9x9 box of random letters is read from a file. The program goes through the box and displays all the words that exist in the box, regardless of them being forwards or backward in every row, column and diagonal. The GitHub repositories contain all the code for Sudoku and Word Search.

Standford Open Datathon

I participated in Stanford Open Datathon 2020, in which my team and I conducted a reasearch on the future of online education in a post-pandemic Pakistan. We took a survey from a substantial number of people and wrote code that scrapes Twitter for all the tweets posted in Pakistan that mention online education. Then the tweets are performed a sentimental analysis on and two separate word-clouds are made, one positive and one negative. The wordclouds are then inferred from to make a hypothesis on the problems online education faces in Pakistan and their solutions. Single Cycle Datapath

The Single Cycle Datapath was made as the project for my Computer Organization and Assembly Language course. In Logisim, I created a Data Path consisting of a RAM, an ALU and a Control Unit to perform a heap of logical and arithmetic functions on any set of values, store the outputs and retreive them at will. All the circuits and sub-curcuits such as Binary Cells, Multiplexers, Demultiplexers, Decoders, Encoders and Binary Adders were made from scratch and not used from Logisim's list of built-in components.

Made by Ahmad Saad using Boostrap 5
